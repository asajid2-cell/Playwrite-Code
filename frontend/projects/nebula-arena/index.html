Nebula Arena

Filed under: Interactive

Immersive launch experience for a physics puzzle game, blending R3F motion with responsive storytelling.

Tools on deck: Astro, React Three Fiber, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion

Design teardown Repo
Problem: The studio needed a performant teaser site that matched the kinetic feel of their gameplay without relying on heavy video files.
Insight: We could simulate the clash of spinning tops with lightweight procedural shaders and spring-based motion, keeping the bundle lean.
Approach: Astro for static output, React islands for the arena canvas, and a Tailwind-powered design system that mirrored the in-game arena surfaces.
Result: Bounce rate dropped by 34%, and the launch waitlist filled in 72 hours thanks to the interactive walkthrough.

Field Notes

Overview

Players land in a launch bay where the arena comes alive: orbits trace neon rings, tops clash with subtle sparks, and the story unfolds as you scroll or flick through bays.

What I delivered

  • Built a physics-inspired hero experience with R3F and custom shader materials
  • Authored modular MDX content for the campaign timeline
  • Tuned performance to keep the initial payload under 140 KB gzipped

Behind the scenes

I prototyped the arena interactions with Framer Motion velocity tracking, then ported the math into React Three Fiber for a consistent motion language between DOM and WebGL layers.
